What is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a three-digit number that represents your creditworthiness. It typically ranges from 300 to 900, with higher scores indicating better financial health. Lenders use credit scores to determine whether to approve loans, set interest rates, and offer credit limits.

Importance of Credit Score

  • Easier Loan Approvals: Banks and financial institutions prefer borrowers with high credit scores.
  • Lower Interest Rates: A better score can help you secure loans at lower interest rates.
  • Higher Credit Card Limits: A strong score allows you to access higher credit limits.
  • Better Rental & Employment Opportunities: Some landlords and employers check credit scores before approving tenants or hiring employees.
  • Financial Stability & Security: A good score helps you access credit when needed without hassle.

Understanding Credit Score Ranges

Credit Score Range Meaning Impact
750 - 900 Excellent Easily approved for loans with the best interest rates.
650 - 749 Good Eligible for loans with competitive interest rates.
550 - 649 Fair Might get approved but with higher interest rates.
300 - 549 Poor High risk; likely to face loan rejections.
